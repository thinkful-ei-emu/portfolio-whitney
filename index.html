About Me

As a teacher, planning math lessons was a bit like trying to figure out how to teach a butterfly why the sky is blue. These little eight year olds didn't care about abstract algebraic concepts! They just wanted to flutter their wings in the concrete world.

While searching for ways to tangibly express these concepts, I would often find coding-esque examples. I became instantly intrigued and began doing some small coding lessons in my spare time.

It was instant brain food! It satiated my hunger for solving puzzles, cracking codes, and taking things apart and putting them back together again. The rest, as they say, is history!

Currently, I am a student in the Engineering Immersion Program bootcamp at Thinkful. I love coding because it's a visual representation of math and logic working together to make something beautiful.

When I'm not coding, you can find me playing with my inexhaustible dogs, creating with polymer clay, or re-watching the original Star Wars trilogy (or anything to do with space). I also love learning new things! My current learning goals, other than learning to code, include working with resin and learning to play Dungeons & Dragons.
